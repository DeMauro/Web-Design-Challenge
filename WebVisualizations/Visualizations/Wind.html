Wind Speed

The breeze seems consistent across the globe, with most cities within the 0-10 mph zone. Higher outliers may be due to storms. It is of note that the highest wind speed appears to be closest to the coldest regions; the strongest southern winds are closest to the Antarctic circle (66.5 degrees south) and the strongest northern winds are closest to the Arctic circle (66.5 degrees north).

Interestingly, wind speed decreases as one travels from the Antarctic circle to the equator (weak downhill regression in Southern hemisphere) and increases again as one travels North to the Arctic circle (weak uphill regression in Northern hemisphere). It gets windier as one moves away from the equator.
